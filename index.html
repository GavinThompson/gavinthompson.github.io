iamSick.ca

iamSick is a startup focused on helping patients find care, when and where they need it. I worked with the iamSick team through 2013 as their sole developer and rebuilt an early prototype of the platform using Ruby on Rails with heavy integration of the Google Maps API.

During my time there, I implemented major features targeted at improving accessibility for non-english speaking patients in Canada. These features included the filtering of services by languages provided by each medical facility.

Cool Shit, Good People -- The Platform

I wanted to create a platform to highlight and showcase incredible people who are building incredible things. With our incredibly fast 24-hour news cycle, good news often gets buried, if it gets any coverage at all. I wanted to build a platform which stores and highlights individuals making the world a much better place.

Built using MeteorJS, the original intention was to allow users to come to the site and post their own links. The project evolved in April 2017, now located at a private URL and solely acts as an API for a Chrome extension. Highlighted below, the extension is much more accesible to users and replaces the Facebook trending feed with the posts found on the platform. Currently 900+ posts shared and counting!

Cool Shit, Good People -- Chrome Extension

As mentioned above, I built a Chrome extension! It takes all of the posts from the Cool Shit, Good People platform and replaces the Facebook "trending" section with these custom links of amazing people building and doing incredible things.

While I was very proud of the MeteorJS platform, I found this extension made the posts much more accessible and removed the need for editorial oversight on the platform. The extension displays most recent, most popular, most visited, and random posts. It was built using a little bit of love and a whole lot of JavaScript wizardry!

Yourem Ipsum

Several years ago lorem ipsum generators were incredibly popular. Ipsums like Bacon Ipsum, Cat Ipsum, and Samuel L Ipsum were all great if you needed some filler text. I thought there might be a niche need for copywriters and designers to have the ability to generate their own, personal ipsums.

The project was originally built with MeteorJS. Users could upload a CSV list of words and phrases and then it would regurgitate a custom number of randomized Ipsum paragraphs. Lists were saved on the platform for future use, and paragraphs could be generated with words "as is," or words could go through a transformation resembling a parody of lorem ipsum where english words meet broken-latin suffixes.
