Cool Shit, Good People -- The Platform

I wanted to create a platform to highlight and showcase incredible people who are building incredible things. With our incredibly fast 24-hour news cycle, truly good news often gets buried, if it gets an exposure at all. I wanted to build a platform which stores and highlights individuals making the world a much better place.

Built using MeteorJS the original intention, was to allow anyone to come to the site and post their own links. The project changed in April 2017. The project is now located at a private URL and solely acts as an API for a Chrome extension which replaces the Facebook trending feed. Currently 900+ posts shared and counting!

Cool Shit, Good People -- Chrome Extension

As mentioned above, I built a Chrome extension! It takes all of the posts from the Cool Shit, Good People platform and replaces the Facebook "Trending" section with these custom links of amazing people building and doing incredible things.

While I was very proud of the MeteorJS platform, I found this extension made the news much more accessible and removed the need for editorial oversight on the quality of the links posted to the platform. The extension displays most recent, most popular, most visited, or random posts. It was built using a little bit of love and a whole lot of JavaScript wizardry.

One Minute Mandalas

Originally designed as a room-scale interactive installation using projectors, this project was downsized to be more accessible to anyone with a computer or a smartphone. I'm glad I did as feedback has been very positive. ðŸ˜Š

There's a line that keeps getting thrown around within the tech/startup/developer community and it focuses on "build what you will use." In 2016, I had begun to spend some time experimenting with meditation. I was inspired to build an installation that allowed participants to submit a thought, or a wish, or anything they wanted to "release" into the universe -- and from that a unique mandala would be generated on the wall. I found the repeating patterns of the mandala and the hypnotic music was the perfect tool to get into the zen-like meditation mindset.

One Minute Mandalas was built with P5js, a JavaScript library which reinterprets the Processing language for the web.

Yourem Ipsum

Several years ago lorem ipsum generators were becoming incredibly popular. Ipsums like Bacon Ipsum, Cat Ipsum, and Samuel L Ipsum were all great if you needed some filler text. Hopping on the trend I wondered why couldn't people or companies just create their own ipsums?

Yourem Ipsum was designed to be everyone's personal Ipsum generator. I thought it would be handy for companies and copy editors to be able to create ipsums based on their current clients or project needs. You could provide the platform with a CSV list of words and phrases and then it would provide however many randomized Ipsum paragraphs you needed. Lists were saved on the platform for future use, and you could generate "normal" words or words that went through a pseudo-latinification process.
